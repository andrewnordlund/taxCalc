Do you have a workplace pension but still want to invest to supplement that, and you're not sure if you should invest in an RRSP, or keep your investments in an unregistered account? The RRSP will give you tax-back today at your marginal rate, but you'll pay tax on your withdrawals at your retirement marginal rate. Okay, but what if you take a gap-year between retirement and collecting a pension? You could withdraw your whole RRSP and pay your average tax rate on that; then collect your pension the next year. What about that Scenario?

Check out the Year by Year RRSP vs Uniregistered Retirement Savings Calculator to answer questions like these.
